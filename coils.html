Chilled Water or Direct Expansion?

The chilled water coil circulates cold water (typically 7°C supply, 12°C return) through copper tubes. Air passes across the finned surface, cooling and dehumidifying as it goes. Used in all central plant systems — chiller + AHU configurations in offices, hospitals, hotels, and data centres.

Coils supplied to other HVAC manufacturers for integration into their own AHU or air handling products.

The direct expansion coil evaporates refrigerant directly inside the copper tubes — no chilled water circuit required. The refrigerant enters as a liquid through a distributor, evaporates across the coil, and exits as a superheated gas. Used in split systems, packaged units, and close-control air conditioning where a chiller plant is not available.

Multi-Refrigerant Compatible

DX coils are designed for R410A, R32, or R407C as standard. Legacy R22 coils also available for replacement applications. Refrigerant compatibility stated on the data plate.

🔀

Balanced Distribution

Venturi-type distributor with individual capillary tubes to each circuit ensures balanced refrigerant distribution across the coil face — maximising evaporator efficiency.

✂️

Face-Split Interlacing

Face-split or row-split coils allow two independent refrigerant circuits in a single coil body — for parallel compressor staging or hot gas bypass applications.

🔧

Brazed Connections

All refrigerant connections are brazed — not flared. Nitrogen-purged during brazing to prevent internal oxidation. Supplied with protective end-caps for transit.
