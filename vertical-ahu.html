Single Skin or Double Skin?

The single skin vertical AHU uses 0.8mm GI casing in a draw-through vertical arrangement. The fan sits at the top, drawing air upward through the filter and coil sections below. Lower first cost than double skin; suited to plant rooms and service areas where thermal or acoustic casing performance is not critical.

Minimal Floor Footprint

Vertical arrangement stacks sections on a small base footprint — critical for plant rooms with limited floor space but adequate height clearance.

Cost-Effective

GI single skin construction keeps first cost lower than double skin equivalent while delivering the same airside performance.

Draw-Through Efficiency

Fan at the top draws air through the heat exchanger — provides more uniform airflow across the coil face compared to blow-through configurations.

🔧

Side-Access Panels

All sections accessed from the side. Filter and coil servicing without requiring overhead access. Suitable for low-ceiling plant rooms.

The double skin vertical AHU uses the same PUF sandwich panel and thermally broken aluminium frame system as the full horizontal AHU range — applied in a vertical draw-through arrangement. Delivers class L2 casing leakage, no condensation on the casing, and significantly reduced break-out noise. The choice for conditioned buildings and acoustic-sensitive locations.
